An overarching focus of my time at Texas Tech University was to build awareness—whether through maps, statistical analyses or direct faculty outreach—of the distinct overlap between TTU’s rural West Texas setting and national trends towards rural brain drain stagnation and population loss. From this foundation, I helped build a series of interventions and a proposed package of scholarship support successfully funded under the Type II track of the NSF S-STEM program, bringing together faculty from five STEM fields and the College of Education for this five-year project beginning in Spring 2020


English Opens Doors, a joint program of the Chilean Ministry of Education and the United Nations Development Program, seeks to place native English speakers in Chilean public schools. Volunteering for Liceo Experimental UMAG in Punta Arenas, Chile, performing independent and team-teaching for students ranging in age from the 5th to the 12th grade.
